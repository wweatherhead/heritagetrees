Heritage Tree Walking Tour

Welcome to Forest Hill Cemetery, just on the outskirts of Madison, WI. Forest Hill is the burial site of many famous Wisconsin residents, but more importantly it is the home for seven heritage oaks. Heritage oaks are oak trees from any species that are more than two hundred years old! There are heritage oaks all over Madison, and the cataloging of these trees started in 1976 thanks to conservationist Walter Scott as a means to preserve “a living link with our past”. The name “heritage oak” is an allusion to the two hundredth anniversary of the United States, a date that connotes a sense of pride in the legacy that these trees and the United States alike have wrought. Indeed that begets a question. Would anybody care at all if the significance of our country’s legacy was not connected? How does legacy influence memory, and how do we as humans decide what gets to be remembered? On this walking tour, reflect on these questions and more as you stroll through history.
